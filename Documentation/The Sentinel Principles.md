# 🛡️ The Sentinel Principles

> Engineering Principles of Project Sentinel

---

## Purpose

Project Sentinel is a Cybersecurity Engineering Workspace designed to transform knowledge into engineering practice.

More than a study repository, it serves as an engineering environment where laboratory work, technical documentation, automation, architecture decisions and real-world cybersecurity projects coexist as part of a continuous learning journey.

The purpose of this workspace is not to collect information, but to develop capabilities. Every document, script, playbook and experiment should contribute to becoming a better cybersecurity engineer.

If someone asks, "What is Project Sentinel?", the answer should be simple:

> It is the engineering workspace of a cybersecurity professional who builds, tests, documents and continuously improves security solutions.

---

## Vision

Project Sentinel is expected to evolve alongside its owner.

Every commit should represent an engineering decision.

Every directory should represent a real capability.

Every document should preserve knowledge that would otherwise be lost.

The ultimate goal is to build a living engineering workspace capable of supporting long-term professional growth while serving as tangible evidence of technical competence.

Engineering excellence is measured not by accumulated knowledge, but by reproducible capability.

---

## Principles

### Clarity before Complexity

Engineering solutions should remain understandable. Future maintenance is as important as present implementation.

### Purpose before Accumulation

Nothing belongs in this workspace without a clear engineering purpose. Every artifact should contribute to detection, response, automation, hardening, research or operational improvement.

### Documentation is Engineering

Documentation is not an afterthought. Well-documented systems are maintainable systems.

### Security by Default

Security principles should guide every technical decision, even inside laboratory environments.

### Traceable Decisions

Technical decisions should always be explainable. The reasoning behind an implementation is often more valuable than the implementation itself.

### Simplicity over Novelty

New technologies are adopted because they solve real problems, not because they are popular.

---

## Engineering Mindset

Project Sentinel adopts the mindset of an engineer rather than that of a student.

Ideas are validated through experimentation.

Laboratories are treated as production-like environments whenever possible.

Frameworks and tools are understood before being adopted.

Every implementation should answer practical engineering questions rather than simply demonstrate technical knowledge.

Curiosity, critical thinking and continuous improvement guide every project developed within this workspace.

---

## Decision Making

Technical decisions are driven by engineering value rather than technological trends.

New tools, certifications and technologies are evaluated according to the capabilities they provide, considering factors such as operational impact, maintainability, visibility, automation and long-term sustainability.

Whenever appropriate, important architectural decisions should be documented through Architecture Decision Records (ADRs), preserving both the decision and the reasoning behind it.

---

## Continuous Learning

Continuous learning is the engine that drives Project Sentinel.

However, learning is only considered complete when knowledge becomes reusable.

Every new capability should ideally result in one or more engineering assets, such as:

- Technical documentation
- Laboratory environments
- Automation scripts
- Playbooks
- Detection rules
- Templates
- Architecture decisions

Success is measured by capabilities acquired, not by courses completed.

---

## Evolution

The Sentinel Principles are intentionally stable.

Technologies, tools, workflows and implementations will naturally evolve over time.

The engineering principles described in this document should evolve only when the philosophy of Project Sentinel itself changes.
